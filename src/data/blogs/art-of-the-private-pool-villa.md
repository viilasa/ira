The private pool has become so common in luxury real estate marketing that it risks becoming invisible — a checklist item rendered in stock photography and forgotten by the time the brochure closes.

We think about it differently at IRA Estates. Not as a feature to be listed, but as a design decision that changes the entire spatial logic of a home.

## Orientation is everything

At IRA Estates in Benaulim, every villa pool is oriented to catch the evening light. This is not accidental. The afternoon sun in Goa is aggressive — by design, the pool becomes usable and genuinely beautiful in the hours when the light turns gold and the heat softens.

The pool deck is not a patio that happens to have water in it. It is a transitional space — between interior and exterior, between the activity of the day and the stillness of evening. The materials (local laterite stone, unpolished) absorb heat gently and release it slowly. The surround is wide enough for two chairs, never wide enough to feel like a hotel forecourt.

## The view axis

Each pool at IRA is positioned on a deliberate view axis. From the main living area, the pool reflects sky. From the pool, the living area frames like a painting. This reciprocity — each space making the other more beautiful — is what distinguishes a designed home from an assembled one.

> "Each space making the other more beautiful — this is what distinguishes a designed home from an assembled one."

There are eight villas in the IRA Estates development. Each pool is identical in dimension. Each is entirely unlike the others in what it sees, what it reflects, and how it sits within its particular landscape.

## Material choices

We use local laterite stone, unpolished, because it ages well and belongs here. We avoid glossy porcelain and imported marble — not because we cannot source them, but because they announce themselves too loudly. A pool surround should fade into the landscape, not compete with it.

The pool interior is finished in a dark grey mosaic that reads almost black in shade and deep blue in direct sun. It does not look like every other pool. It looks like the sea.

That is the art of it.
