# Investing in Goa Real Estate in 2026: What the Numbers Say

Goa's real estate market has been in a sustained expansion since the post-pandemic return of domestic tourism in 2021. What began as a demand spike from remote workers and second-home buyers has matured, over five years, into something more structural: a reorientation of where India's wealthy class chooses to anchor its leisure and its assets.

## The South Goa premium

In 2022, the average transaction price for a luxury villa (4 BHK, private pool, 2000+ sqm plot) in South Goa was ₹3.2 crore. In 2026, comparable properties are transacting at ₹5.1–6.4 crore — a 59–100% appreciation over four years, or roughly **18–24% annualised**.

This is not a bubble. It reflects:

- Real supply constraint (conversion of agricultural land to residential remains tightly regulated)
- Genuine demand from domestic HNIs and NRIs
- A structural shift in how India's upper-middle class thinks about assets

A Goa villa is no longer a vacation indulgence. It is a portfolio decision.

## Rental yield dynamics

Short-stay rental yields on luxury villas in South Goa currently run at **6–9% gross annually**, with peak-season occupancy (November–March) routinely above 85% for well-managed properties. Net yields after management fees and maintenance average 4.5–6.5% — comparable to or better than commercial real estate in Tier 1 cities.

Demand is concentrated in the ₹25,000–60,000 per night segment, which has seen the strongest growth. This is where IRA Estates villas are positioned.

## A note on quality and yield

The most reliable predictor of sustained yield in the Goa luxury market is build quality. Properties that cut corners on finishes, pool design, or landscaping underperform significantly in guest reviews and repeat bookings.
