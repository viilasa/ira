# Why Benaulim? The Story Behind IRA Estates' Location

Site selection in luxury residential development is, at its best, a form of editorial judgment. You are not just finding land. You are committing to a context — a landscape, a community, a set of views and sounds and proximities — that will define the character of every home built there.

We looked at fourteen sites across Goa before we arrived at Benaulim. The shortlist came down to two: a hillside plot near Anjuna with spectacular panoramic views, and a flat coastal site in Benaulim, two minutes from the beach by foot.

## Why we chose the coast

The hillside plot was beautiful. It was also exposed — to wind, to the visibility of neighboring developments, and to the kind of dramatic-but-impractical relationship with landscape that looks better in a render than it feels in daily life.

Benaulim offered something more useful: groundedness. The site is sheltered by mature palms. The land is flat enough that every villa can have a pool at grade. The beach is close enough to hear the sea on still nights, but far enough that the residence has genuine privacy.

The village of Benaulim itself — the church, the market, the fishing community — provides the kind of lived texture that no gated enclave can manufacture. You are in South Goa, not just beside it.

## Access and infrastructure

Dabolim airport is 27 km north. The new Mopa airport extends the catchment further. NH-66 connects the site to Margao (11 km) and Panjim (40 km). Navarchar Hospital and Apollo Clinic are both within 15 minutes.

> "This is a site chosen for the way it feels on the one hundred and first day, not the first."

Within walking distance of the IRA Estates site:

- **Benaulim Beach** — 800 m (2 min walk)
- **Local market** — 600 m
- **Church of St John the Baptist** — 1.1 km
- **Taj Exotica Resort** — 2.4 km
- **Margao Railway Station** — 11 km

That is the IRA Estates difference.
