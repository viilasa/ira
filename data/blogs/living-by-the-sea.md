# Living by the Sea: What South Goa Offers That Nowhere Else Can

There is a Goa that tourists arrive in, and a Goa that residents never leave. South Goa belongs entirely to the second kind.

Benaulim sits at the quieter end of the coastal strip — wide beaches, the smell of salt and laterite, fishing boats pulled up at dawn, and a pace that has resisted every attempt to speed it up. It is not undiscovered. It is simply unmoved.

For those building a home here, that quality is everything. The question is not whether you can find beauty in Benaulim — it is whether you are ready to stop and receive it. The sea does not hurry. The palms do not lean into the wind so much as sway with it. The light at six in the evening is the kind that makes you put down whatever you were holding.

## What makes South Goa different

North Goa is extraordinary in its energy. South Goa is extraordinary in its absence of it. This is not a criticism — it is the whole point. The infrastructure is quieter, the beaches are longer and less trafficked, and the architecture still carries traces of the Portuguese parish towns that once defined this coastline.

For a private residence, this matters enormously. A home is not a hotel. It is the place you return to. And what you return to — the view, the soundscape, the quality of the air — shapes the quality of your days in ways that square footage never can.

## The IRA Estates approach

IRA Estates was founded on the conviction that the finest homes in Goa are not the largest or the most ornamented. They are the ones that know where they are — and let that knowledge inform every decision, from the angle of a window to the material of a wall.

The laterite walls come from local quarries. The rooflines respond to monsoon drainage. The deep verandahs exist because the Goan afternoon demands shade, not because shade is fashionable.
