# Kasauli: India's Next Luxury Hill Retreat

The hill station has been India's summer refuge for two centuries. What has changed in the last decade is who goes, why, and how long they stay.

Shimla and Mussoorie remain popular — and increasingly crowded. The travellers who built those markets are now looking for something quieter, closer, and more considered. Kasauli, 65 km from Chandigarh and a 45-minute drive from the plains, has emerged as the destination that answers this search.

## What Kasauli offers

At 1,800 metres (roughly 6,000 ft), Kasauli sits above the summer heat that makes the foothills uncomfortable from April through June. The town itself is small:

- A colonial-era cantonment
- A church and market square
- A handful of excellent small restaurants
- Pine forest in every direction

The roads are narrow. The construction density is low. The air is clean in a way that residents of Delhi and Chandigarh have largely forgotten is possible.

## Why it hasn't been overdeveloped

Kasauli sits within a cantonment zone, which means development restrictions are significantly tighter than comparable hill towns. This is precisely what keeps it beautiful — and what keeps demand consistently ahead of supply.

> "In the right places, regulation is not an obstacle. It is the moat."

New construction permissions are limited. Quality residential supply is scarce. For the discerning buyer, that scarcity is the investment thesis.
